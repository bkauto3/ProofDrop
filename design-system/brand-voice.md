# ProofDrop — Brand Voice

## Brand Identity

ProofDrop turns cryptographic proof bundles into human-readable receipts. The brand voice reflects the same transformation: technical precision translated into accessible clarity.

---

### Precise
ProofDrop speaks with technical confidence, not vagueness. Names things accurately. Doesn't hedge.
- "Verify your AIVS proof bundle" not "Check your file"
- "SHA-256 content hash" not "unique identifier"
- "PASS: all 7 verification checks passed" not "Looks good!"

### Clear
Every message has one purpose. Remove anything that doesn't serve the reader's task.
- No jargon without explanation
- No five-word when two will do
- Complexity earns its place

### Professional
ProofDrop serves people who invoice for work, submit to compliance teams, and present to legal departments. The tone reflects that trust context.
- Formal but not stiff
- Confident but not arrogant
- Direct, never dismissive

---

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing copy | Confident, clear | "Turn any AIVS proof bundle into a client-ready receipt in seconds" |
| Verification PASS | Affirming, factual | "Verification passed. All 7 checks completed successfully." |
| Verification FAIL | Direct, helpful | "Verification failed. 2 issues found — see details below." |
| Error message | Honest, actionable | "Invalid JSON format. Check your bundle and try again." |
| Empty state | Inviting | "Paste your proof bundle below to get started" |
| Sign-in prompt | Low-pressure | "Sign in to save your receipt history" |
| Pricing | Value-forward | "Permanent history. PDF exports. API access." (features, not hype) |

---

## Vocabulary

### Use
- Proof bundle (not "file", not "document")
- Receipt (not "report", not "certificate")
- Verify (not "validate", not "check")
- PASS / FAIL (always uppercase when referring to status)
- Bundle hash (not "fingerprint", not "checksum")
- Verifier version (not "tool version")

### Avoid
- "Magic" — ProofDrop is technical infrastructure, not magic
- "Amazing", "awesome", "incredible" — reserve real superlatives
- "Don't worry" — condescending in professional context
- Exclamation marks in error messages
- Passive voice in CTAs ("Get receipt generated" → "Generate receipt")

## Microcopy Examples

**Button labels**:
- Primary CTA: "Verify & Generate Receipt"
- Copy action: "Copy receipt URL"
- Export: "Download PDF"
- Billing: "Manage billing"

**Empty states**:
- No receipts yet: "No receipts yet. Verify your first proof bundle to get started."
- No session: "Sign in to view your receipt history"

**Success messages**:
- "Receipt created. Share this URL with your client."
- "Copied to clipboard."

**Error messages**:
- "Invalid JSON. Check your bundle format and try again."
- "Bundle hash not found. This receipt may have been deleted."
- "Verification failed with 2 errors. See details below."
